# Paper ID traceability note

The public master table contains the **71 PRISMA-included studies (Corpus B)** used in the manuscript synthesis.

Two identifiers are provided:

1. **Included Study No. (1–71)**  
   A consecutive index added for transparent included-study accounting in the public release.

2. **Paper ID (P01, P02, …)**  
   A stable registry identifier used throughout the manuscript, tables, figures, and repository.

Why are Paper IDs non-consecutive?
- During screening and coding, a broader registry was maintained for candidate records.
- Stable Paper IDs were assigned before the final PRISMA-included set was frozen.
- Some registry entries were later excluded at full-text eligibility, removed during stabilization, or otherwise not retained in Corpus B.
- To preserve traceability across working files, manuscript tables, and release files, the stable Paper IDs were **not renumbered** after final inclusion.

Therefore:
- **Corpus size should be read from Included Study No. (1–71) or the reported N values**, not from the maximum Paper ID.
- **Paper ID should be used as the stable join/reference key** across S1A, S1B, manuscript tables, and figure citations.
